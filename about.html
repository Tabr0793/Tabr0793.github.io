Hey there!

I'm Taylor

an Architectual Designer gone UI/UX Design motivated to develop thoughtful products; inspired by real people and their needs.

My path to design

I've always been an observant person. Even as a child, I was oddly aware of how my surroundings influenced me. Whether it be landscape, buildings, simple objects, art; I've always been drawn to the detail. As a person who can’t help but expose the raw geometry behind the larger picture, my enthusiasm for reversing the process, developing my own designs, was a no brainer.

Yellow Boat. Traverse City, Michigan 2016

Fun Facts

I am a visual and social learning, which means I love sharing ideas and hearing suggestions from others. I believe there is something to learn behind every persons' story. My most dominant personality trait is empathy. If I couldn’t design, I’d be a psychologist or a surgeon (minus the part where I pass out at the site of blood). Oh, and I’m a twin!

From Architect to Tech

Although I grew up destined to pursue design, I can't say I went to college knowing exactly what I wanted to be. However, I'm grateful that my 18 year old self blindly chose to pursue architecture even though I've chosen a different path today.
During the completion of my architecture training, I developed a solid foundation of basic design fundamentals (spatial balance, elemental relationships, visual hierarchy, and color theory) and learned methods that have proven to be an invaluable part of my human centered design process today.

Woman descending Chase Tower stairs. Chicago, Illinois 2016

After College

Coming from school, where the sky was the limit in the creative realm, I was eager to embrace my education and influence the world through design. After accepting my first job at a commercial architecture firm, it didn't take long before I started to understand the creative restrictions city code requirements and price restraints placed on architects. Gorwing wheery of the muted creativity, and lack of social stimulation, I moved into sales when I was recruited by Emser Tile to be an Architectural Design Representative. I got to pump my creative muscles in a different way by visiting design firms all around the Bay Area and showing others how they could elevate their projects with Emser's products. I was meeting lots of people and having a blast but I couldn't deny the piece of me that needed to design again.

The Leap

Unsure how to pursue my need to design, I made my way up to San Francisco where I discovered the hustle and bustle of the tech world. It was unlike anything I’d seen in my architecture bubble. I loved the energy, the young blood and uncapped possibilities. Expressing my desire to get involved, a couple of tech savvy friends introduced me to UI/UX Design........it instantly struck the perfect chord in my social, creative, order-loving, mind.

I now hold a certificate in UI/UX Design from BLOC!
