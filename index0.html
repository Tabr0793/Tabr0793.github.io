Hey there!

I'm Taylor

a UI/UX Designer motivated to develop thoughtful products; inspired by real people and their needs.

My path to design

Growing up, I always had an undeniable attraction to the world. Whether it be landscape, buildings, objects, art; I was always drawn to the detail. As a person who can’t help but expose the raw geometry behind the larger picture, my enthusiasm for reversing the process, developing my own designs, was a no brainer.

Woman descending Chase Tower stairs. Chicago, Illinois 2016

Fun Facts

I live for moments when a person opens up to tell me their life story. I believe learning from experience and others' experiences is the best way to learn. My most dominant personality trait is empathy. If I couldn’t design, I’d be a psychologist or a surgeon (minus the part where I pass out at the site of lots of blood). Oh, and I’m a twin!

From Architect to Web Designer

Although I grew up destined to pursue design, I can't say I went to college knowing exactly what I wanted to be. However, I'm grateful that my 18 year old self blindly chose to pursue architecture even though I've chosen a different path today.
During the completion of my architecture training, I developed an understanding of basic design fundamentals (spatial balance, elemental relationships, visual hierarchy, and color theory). I also gained experience designing with intention solving a variety of complex problems through communication, comprehensive analysis and design iterations developed for the user.

Woman descending Chase Tower stairs. Chicago, Illinois 2016

Work life

Coming from school, where the sky was the limit in the creative realm, I was eager to take on the world and influence it through design. I was ready to make a difference. When I accepted my first job as a designer at a small commercial architecture firm, it didn't take long before I started to understand the creative restrictions city code requirements and price restraints placed on architects. After a year of built friendships and accomplishments, I decided to take a leap when I was recruited by Emser Tile to be an Architectural Design Representative. I got to pump my creative muscles in a different way by visiting design firms all around the Bay Area and showing others how they could elevate their projects with Emser's products. I was meeting lots of people and having a blast.

The Leap

Thanks to my leap with Emser, I had an opportunity to make my way up to San Francisco. I discovered the city was a whole different experience. Everybody I met seemed to be in the tech industry. The hustle and bustle of the tech world was unlike anything I’d seen in my architecture bubble. I loved the energy, the young blood and uncapped possibilities. Expressing my desire to get involved, a couple of tech savvy friends introduced me to UI/UX Design. It instantly struck the perfect chord in my social, creative, order-loving, mind.
